Our History

UCL Rocket is the newest team under UCL Racing, having been formed in September of 2021, towards the end of the COVID-19 pandemic.

Despite only having run for two years and with the struggles from COVID, we have participated and achieved excellent results in competitions both nationally and internationally!

We won the UK’s National Rocketry Championship (the first and only London team to do so), ranked the 2nd European team at the Spaceport America Cup, the biggest student rocketry competition in the world, beating out over 100 global contenders.

Meliora

Our Meliora competition team is unwaveringly committed to spearheading our most extensive and ambitious projects, whether for competitive endeavors or groundbreaking exploration. Having emerged victorious among a pool of more than 100 contenders at the Spaceport America Cup, we at Meliora have not only met but far exceeded all expectations.

Now, picture us aiming even higher—we're set on being the first European student team to reach space with a bi-propellant liquid rocket engine. As we gear up for EuRoC-25, our record-breaking ambitions remain steadfast as we continue to redefine the bounds of student-led space exploration.

F.I.R.E

At UCL Rocket, we prioritize hands-on learning, embodied by our F.I.R.E competition team, comprising solely First-Year Engineers. From the outset, these students tackle the challenge of manufacturing and competing with their own unique rocket designs.

Despite their limited experience, the F.I.R.E team consistently exceeds expectations, triumphing over senior teams and winning the National Rocketry Championship. Currently, F.I.R.E is focused on advancing their Solid motor technology, with the goal of breaching the Kármán line in the coming years!

R.E.D

The Research Experimental Division (RED) stands as a beacon of innovation, dedicated to crafting cutting-edge contraptions and components that elevate our rockets to new heights. Within R.E.D, members embrace a culture of creativity, nurturing their imaginative prowess to conceive and develop groundbreaking systems in the realm of student-led rocketry.

At present, R.E.D is immersed in the development of an in-house flight computer and precision-controlled airbrakes, a testament to our commitment to reducing costs and unlocking untapped potential in our rocket endeavours.

S.T.A.R.S

Our Specialised Training and Rocketry Skills programme is our latest addition to UCL Rocket. The S.T.A.R.S program, in collaboration with UKRA, offers three certifications, including two for High Power Rocketry and one for Model Rocketry. Through this program, we aim to provide certifications to all aspiring engineers who wish to launch their own amateur rockets.

S.T.A.R.S not only enhances technical expertise but also sparks interest to people of all academic levels through multiple launches, hands-on experiences, and creative freedom, reinforcing our commitment to a dynamic UCL Rocket community.
